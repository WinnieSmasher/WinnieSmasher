### About

I am a graduate researcher at the Space Science Institute, Macau University of Science and Technology. My work sits around space science data, computer vision, Python research tooling, and practical data systems.

I like building things that can survive contact with imperfect data: clear pipelines, reproducible experiments, conservative assumptions, and tools that make hidden data problems visible before they become fake confidence.

Current interests:

- Space science data analysis and lunar / planetary computer vision
- Python research tooling, data cleaning, and experiment pipelines
- Low-frequency A-share quant research for small personal capital
- Applied AI systems, automation, and reverse-engineering-adjacent tooling

### Research Taste

I prefer boring, testable systems over impressive-looking claims. For research and quant work, I treat free data as useful but untrusted: collect it cheaply, clean it carefully, compare it when possible, and never let a public factor become a trading decision without human reasoning.
